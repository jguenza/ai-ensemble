AI-Ensemble

A three-stage reasoning system based on Scenario, Ensemble, and Compiler.

Overview

AI-Ensemble is a structured reasoning system designed to support complex problem-solving, learning, and decision-making.

Instead of interacting with a generic chatbot, users work through a fixed reasoning pipeline that separates problem specification, alternative reasoning, and grounded synthesis.

The system is available both as an online, scenario-driven learning environment and as a fully local, low-carbon (“Green AI”) version that runs entirely on the user’s own machine.

Both versions share the same core principles: controlled reasoning, explicit knowledge inputs, and reliability over fluency.

The Reasoning Pipeline

At the core of AI-Ensemble is a fixed three-stage pipeline designed to mirror how real technical and strategic decisions are made.

Scenario — prompt specification

This stage focuses on precisely defining the problem to be addressed, including context, constraints, assumptions, and objectives.

Scenarios may involve tasks such as:

  • writing or reviewing code
  • planning an experiment
  • improving rate of genetic gain
  • optimizing breeding strategies
  • ICT or process optimization

Poorly specified problems are treated as a first-class failure mode.

Ensemble — alternative reasoning

This stage generates multiple alternative answers or reasoning paths for the same prompt.

Its purpose is to expose uncertainty, disagreement, and competing assumptions before any synthesis or decision is made.

Compiler — grounded synthesis

This stage compares and reconciles alternatives produced by the Ensemble.

It applies curated knowledge and explicit constraints to produce a defensible result, or to state clearly when no reliable conclusion can be drawn.

Why Three Stages Matter

Most AI systems merge all reasoning into a single response. AI-Ensemble deliberately separates stages of reasoning.

This separation reduces confusion, exposes hidden trade-offs, prevents premature conclusions, and mirrors how real projects are run.

The stages work together, but they never collapse into one.

User-Defined Scenarios and Private Knowledge

Users are not limited to predefined scenarios. They can create their own, choose which information is available, and control how reasoning is performed.

Knowledge is not global by default. AI-Ensemble operates on private, user-provided knowledge sources. If information is not provided, the system does not invent it.

Local “Green AI” Version

The local version runs entirely on the user’s own machine and is designed for efficiency, privacy, and independence.

  • CPU-only (no GPU required)
  • Runs on commodity hardware (4–8 cores, 16–32 GB RAM)
  • Uses quantized models
  • No cloud dependency or external data access

In the local version, the Ensemble stage can be implemented using multiple small, quantized models running entirely on the user’s machine.

Design Philosophy

AI-Ensemble is built around a simple principle:

Reliable reasoning emerges from structure, constraints, and explicit knowledge — not from scale alone.
