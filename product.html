AI-Ensemble

A triple-screen reasoning system for scenario-based learning, expert analysis, and managerial decision support.

Overview

AI-Ensemble is a role-based reasoning system designed to support complex problem-solving, learning, and decision-making.

Instead of interacting with a generic chatbot, users work through a structured, three-screen interface that separates problem framing, expert reasoning, and managerial decision support.

The system is available both as an online, scenario-driven learning environment and as a fully local, low-carbon (“Green AI”) version that runs entirely on the user’s own machine.

Both versions share the same core principles: controlled reasoning, explicit knowledge inputs, and reliability over fluency.

The Three-Screen Reasoning Model

At the core of AI-Ensemble is a fixed three-screen layout, designed to mirror how real expert decisions are made.

Scenario Screen

The Scenario screen defines the problem space.

Here, the user formulates a concrete scenario such as:

  • writing or reviewing code
  • planning an experiment
  • improving rate of genetic gain
  • optimizing breeding strategies
  • ICT or process optimization

The scenario establishes objectives, constraints, assumptions, and success criteria. Scenarios may be predefined or fully user-defined.

Expert Screen

The Expert screen supports the scenario with technical reasoning.

  • analyzes methods and options
  • reasons over evidence and domain knowledge
  • highlights assumptions and limitations

This screen represents what is technically sound, not what is operationally or politically convenient.

Manager Screen

The Manager screen focuses on decisions and trade-offs.

  • weighs risks and benefits
  • considers timelines, resources, and feasibility
  • translates expert insight into actionable choices
  • identifies decision bottlenecks

It interprets expertise in context rather than replacing it.

Why Three Screens Matter

Most AI systems merge all reasoning into a single response. AI-Ensemble deliberately separates reasoning perspectives.

This separation reduces confusion, exposes hidden trade-offs, prevents premature conclusions, and mirrors how real projects are run.

The three screens work together, but they never collapse into one.

User-Defined Scenarios and Private Knowledge

Users are not limited to predefined scenarios. They can create their own, choose which information is available, and decide how expert and managerial reasoning should support them.

Knowledge is not global by default. AI-Ensemble operates on private, user-provided knowledge sources. If information is not provided, the system does not invent it.

Local “Green AI” Version

The local version runs entirely on the user’s own machine and is designed for efficiency, privacy, and independence.

  • CPU-only (no GPU required)
  • Runs on commodity hardware (4–8 cores, 16–32 GB RAM)
  • Uses quantized models
  • No cloud dependency or external data access

This version is suitable for schools, families, private research, privacy-sensitive environments, and offline use.

Design Philosophy

AI-Ensemble is built around a simple principle:

Reliable reasoning emerges from structure, constraints, and explicit knowledge — not from scale alone.
